Experimental Linguistics and Fieldwork Lab @ UBC Linguistics

Welcome to the homepage of ELF-Lab at UBC!

The UBC Experimental Linguistics and Fieldwork Lab (ELF-Lab; Directed by Professor Christopher Hammerly) seeks to bring experimental and computational techniques such as eye tracking to fieldwork settings around the world. The aim of the lab is to produce findings applicable to answering theoretical questions within linguistics and psychology, as well as to boosting language reclamation and documentation efforts of Indigenous languages of North America and beyond.

Diederichsen wins Best Oral Presentation Award at LSURC

Undergraduate RA Matthias Diederichsen won the Best Oral Presentation Award for his talk at the Language Sciences Undergraduate Research Coference (LSURC) at UBC, which took place on February 27th and 28th, 2026. His talk was entitled Word Order in a “Free Word Order” Language: An Ojibwe Treebank Study and looked at how animacy impacts word order and argument realization in Ojibwe texts.
